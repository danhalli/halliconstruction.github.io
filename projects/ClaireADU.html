Garage-to-ADU Conversion Roadmap: Portland 2026

Phase 1: The Administrative Intake

The DevHub Account

The Owner needs to create a profile on the City of Portland’s DevHub portal. This is the digital command center where all plans are uploaded and where the city will send corrections or approvals.

DevHub Portal:
https://devhub.portlandoregon.gov/

The Owner Doing Work Statement

Because the Owner is performing the electrical and plumbing labor, Oregon law requires them to sign a specific affidavit. This confirms they are the property owner and will be the one physically doing the trade work, allowing them to bypass the requirement for a separate plumbing or electrical contractor license.

Owner Doing Work Statement (PDF):
https://www.portland.gov/ppd/documents/owner-doing-work-form-property-owner-statement-regarding-construction/download

The SDC Waiver (The Tax Break)

Portland charges System Development Charges (SDCs) to cover the impact a new unit has on city parks, transit, and sewers. For an ADU, these fees usually total $12,000 to $20,000.

The Deal: The city will waive these fees entirely if the Owner signs a Covenant promising not to use the ADU as a short-term rental for 10 years.

The Process: This form must be signed in front of a Notary Public and then recorded with the County before the permit is issued.

SDC Waiver Program Information:
https://www.portland.gov/bds/77447

Phase 2: The Rough-In and Trade Labor

The Structural Shell

Contractor builds the foundation, walls, and exterior shell. Once the shell is up but the interior studs are still exposed, the Owner steps in.

Running the Lines

The Owner installs the wiring, plumbing pipes, and HVAC ducting inside the open walls, with consultation by the contractor.

The Critical Inspection

Before any insulation or drywall can be installed, the Owner must schedule a Rough-In inspection. A city inspector verifies that the plumbing and electrical work meets current Oregon Residential Specialty Code requirements.

The Contractor cannot close the walls until this inspection is approved.

Phase 3: Final Completion and Occupancy

Closing the Walls

After the Owner’s trade work passes inspection, the Contractor installs insulation and drywall.

Trim and Fixtures

The Owner installs finish items including outlets, light fixtures, sinks, and toilets.

Final Inspection

The city performs a final walkthrough of the entire project. Once approved, the Owner receives a Certificate of Occupancy, making the unit legally livable.

3. Estimated City Fees (Portland 2026)

  • Building Permit and Plan Review: $1,500 – $2,500
  • Electrical Permit (Owner-Builder): approximately $165
  • Plumbing Permit (Owner-Builder): $350 – $500
  • Mechanical Permit (Owner-Builder): $150 – $250
  • System Development Charges: $0 if the SDC Waiver is signed and recorded

4. Key 2026 Regulatory Notes

Residency Requirement:
Per ORS 479.540, a homeowner may only perform their own electrical and plumbing work if they own the home and intend to reside in the unit.
Egress Compliance:
Sleeping areas must have at least one window with a clear opening of 5.7 square feet and a sill height no higher than 44 inches from the floor.
Fire Separation:
If the garage is within 3 feet of the property line, the interior wall must be upgraded to a 1-hour fire-rated assembly.
