Garage-to-ADU Conversion Roadmap: Portland 2026

Phase 1: The Administrative Intake

The DevHub Account

The Owner needs to create a profile on the City of Portland’s DevHub portal. This is the digital command center where all plans are uploaded and where the City sends corrections, approvals, inspection scheduling, and invoices.

DevHub Portal:
https://devhub.portlandoregon.gov/

The Owner Doing Work Statement

Because the Owner is performing electrical and plumbing labor, Oregon law requires a signed affidavit confirming ownership and personal performance of the trade work.

Form (PDF):
https://www.portland.gov/ppd/documents/owner-doing-work-form-property-owner-statement-regarding-construction/download

The SDC Waiver and Covenant

Portland charges System Development Charges (SDCs) to offset the impact of new dwelling units on city infrastructure. For ADUs, these typically total $12,000 to $20,000.

The City will waive these fees if the Owner signs and records a covenant agreeing not to use the ADU as a short-term rental for ten years.

Program Information:
https://www.portland.gov/bds/77447

2. Mandatory Forms and Direct Links

Residential Combination Building Permit Application

Primary application bundling building, electrical, and plumbing permits.

https://www.portland.gov/ppd/documents/building-permit-application-building-site-development-demolition-and-zoning-permits/download

Oregon Energy Code Selection – Measure A

Declares insulation and window performance requirements for the conversion.

https://www.oregon.gov/bcd/codes-stand/Documents/energy-code-residential.pdf

Simple Site Plan and Erosion Control

Required when trenching for sewer, water, or electrical service.

https://www.portland.gov/ppd/building-and-zoning-permit-application

Phase 2: The Rough-In and Trade Labor

The Structural Shell

Contractor builds the foundation, walls, and exterior shell. Interior framing remains exposed for trade installation.

Running the Lines

The Owner installs electrical wiring, plumbing piping, and HVAC systems within open walls, with consultation from the Contractor.

The Critical Rough-In Inspection

Before insulation or drywall installation, the Owner must schedule and pass a City rough-in inspection verifying code compliance.

Phase 3: Final Completion and Occupancy

Closing the Walls

After trade approval, the Contractor installs insulation and drywall.

Trim and Fixtures

The Owner installs outlets, lighting, sinks, toilets, and final fixtures.

Final Inspection

Following final approval, the City issues a Certificate of Occupancy.

4. Key 2026 Regulatory Notes

Residency Requirement:
Homeowners may perform their own electrical and plumbing work only if they own and reside in the unit.
Egress Compliance:
Sleeping areas require at least one egress window with a minimum 5.7 square foot clear opening and a sill height no greater than 44 inches.
Fire Separation:
Structures within three feet of a property line require a one-hour fire-rated wall assembly.
