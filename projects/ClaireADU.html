Garage-to-ADU Conversion Roadmap

Location: Portland, Oregon  |  Target Code Year: 2026

Phase 1: Administrative Intake

DevHub Account

The Owner must create a profile on the City of Portland’s DevHub portal. This platform is the official system for plan uploads, corrections, approvals, and permit payments.

Owner Doing Work Statement

Because the Owner is performing the electrical and plumbing labor, Oregon law requires a signed affidavit confirming:

  • The Owner holds title to the property
  • The Owner will personally perform the trade work
  • No separate trade contractor license is required

SDC Waiver & Covenant (Critical Cost Savings)

Portland normally assesses System Development Charges (SDCs) totaling approximately $12,000–$20,000 for a new dwelling unit.

These fees are waived entirely for ADUs if the Owner signs and records a covenant agreeing not to use the unit as a short-term rental (e.g., Airbnb) for 10 years.

Process: Notarization → County recording → Permit issuance

Phase 2: Rough-In & Trade Labor

Structural Shell

The Contractor completes the foundation, framing, windows, and exterior enclosure. Interior wall framing remains exposed.

Owner Trade Work

The Owner installs all electrical wiring, plumbing lines, and HVAC systems within the open walls, with layout consultation from the Contractor as needed.

Rough-In Inspection (Mandatory Hold Point)

Before insulation or drywall may be installed, the Owner must schedule and pass city rough-in inspections.

The Contractor may not close walls until the inspector approves all Owner-installed trade work.

Phase 3: Final Completion & Occupancy

Wall Closure

Following inspection approval, the Contractor installs insulation and drywall.

Finish Work

The Owner installs finish fixtures including outlets, switches, lighting, plumbing fixtures, and appliances.

Final Inspection & Certificate of Occupancy

The city conducts a final inspection of all work. Upon approval, a Certificate of Occupancy is issued, making the ADU legally habitable.

Key Regulatory Notes (2026)

Owner-Builder Eligibility:
Oregon law permits homeowner-performed electrical and plumbing work only when the owner occupies the unit.
Egress:
Sleeping areas require a compliant egress window (minimum 5.7 sq ft clear opening; sill ≤ 44 inches).
Fire Separation:
Garages within 3 feet of a property line require a 1-hour fire-rated wall assembly.
