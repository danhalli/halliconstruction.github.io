Garage-to-ADU Conversion Roadmap

Portland, Oregon · 2026 Code Cycle

Phase 1: Administrative Intake

DevHub Account

The Owner must create a profile on the City of Portland’s Development Hub (DevHub). This is the official system for plan uploads, corrections, approvals, inspection scheduling, and permit payments.

Access Development Hub PDX

Owner Doing Work Statement

Because the Owner is performing the electrical and plumbing labor, Oregon law requires a signed affidavit confirming that the Owner holds title to the property and will personally perform the work.

Download Owner Doing Work Statement (PDF)

SDC Waiver & Covenant

Portland normally assesses System Development Charges (SDCs) totaling approximately $12,000–$20,000 for a new dwelling unit.

These fees are waived for Accessory Dwelling Units if the Owner signs and records a covenant agreeing not to use the unit as a short-term rental (such as Airbnb) for a minimum of 10 years.

Phase 2: Rough-In & Trade Labor

Structural Shell

The Contractor completes foundation, framing, windows, and exterior enclosure while leaving interior walls open.

Owner Trade Work

The Owner installs all electrical wiring, plumbing lines, and HVAC systems inside open walls, with contractor consultation as needed.

Rough-In Inspection (Mandatory Hold Point)

All trade work must pass city rough-in inspections before insulation or drywall may be installed. Walls may not be closed until approval is granted.

Phase 3: Final Completion & Occupancy

Wall Closure

Contractor installs insulation and drywall after rough-in inspection approval.

Finish Work

Owner installs finish fixtures including outlets, switches, lighting, plumbing fixtures, and appliances.

Final Inspection & Certificate of Occupancy

Upon final approval, the City issues a Certificate of Occupancy, making the ADU legally habitable.

Key Regulatory Notes (2026)

Owner-Builder Eligibility:
Oregon law allows homeowners to perform their own electrical and plumbing work only if they own and reside in the unit.
Egress Requirements:
Sleeping areas must include at least one compliant egress window with a minimum 5.7 sq ft clear opening and a sill height no greater than 44 inches.
Fire Separation:
If the structure is within 3 feet of a property line, interior walls must be upgraded to a 1-hour fire-rated assembly.
