Portland, Oregon - 2026 Code Cycle

Phase 1: Administrative Intake

DevHub Account

The owner must create an account on the City of Portland Development Hub. This portal is used for plan uploads, reviews, approvals, inspections, and payments.

Access Development Hub PDX

Owner Doing Work Statement

When the owner performs their own electrical or plumbing work, Oregon law requires a signed affidavit confirming ownership and personal performance.

Download Owner Doing Work Statement (PDF)

SDC Waiver and Covenant

System Development Charges normally total between $12000 and $20000 for a new dwelling unit.

These fees are waived for ADUs if the owner signs and records a covenant agreeing not to use the unit as a short term rental for 10 years.

Notarization, county recording, then permit issuance.

Phase 2: Rough In and Trade Labor

Structural Shell

The contractor completes foundation, framing, windows, and enclosure while interior walls remain open.

Owner Trade Work

The owner installs electrical wiring, plumbing lines, and HVAC systems inside open walls with contractor consultation as needed.

Rough In Inspection

All trade work must pass city inspections before insulation or drywall may be installed.

Phase 3: Final Completion and Occupancy

Wall Closure

The contractor installs insulation and drywall after inspection approval.

Finish Work

The owner installs finish outlets, switches, lights, and plumbing fixtures.

Final Inspection

After approval, the city issues a Certificate of Occupancy.

Key Regulatory Notes

Owner Builder Rule:
Homeowners may perform their own electrical and plumbing work only if they own and occupy the unit.
Egress:
Sleeping areas require at least one compliant egress window.
Fire Separation:
Structures within three feet of a property line require a one hour fire rated wall.
