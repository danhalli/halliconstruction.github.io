Carrie’s Garage-to-ADU Conversion Roadmap

Portland 2026 Code Compliant

Project Overview

This document outlines the permitting, construction, and inspection process for converting an existing garage into a legal Accessory Dwelling Unit (ADU) in Portland, Oregon.

The project is structured so the Owner is the permit holder for administrative and trade-related items, while Halli Construction performs all structural work, coordination, and final finishes. This roadmap is intended to clearly explain the process and responsibilities without overcomplicating or misrepresenting the work.

Phase 1: Administrative Intake

DevHub Account

The Owner creates and maintains a profile in the City of Portland’s DevHub system. This is where plans are submitted, reviews are tracked, and approvals are issued.

https://devhub.portlandoregon.gov/

Owner-Builder Acknowledgments

Because permits are issued under an Owner-Builder structure, the Owner must sign the required acknowledgment forms. These forms confirm responsibility for the permit, not the performance of any specific task.

Owner Doing Work / Owner-Builder Statement (PDF)

SDC Waiver

System Development Charges (SDCs) for an ADU can range from $12,000–$20,000. The City of Portland offers a full waiver if the Owner agrees not to use the ADU as a short-term rental for 10 years.

SDC Waiver Program Information

Critical Requirement:
The SDC Waiver Covenant must be signed, notarized, and recorded. Permits cannot be issued until proof of recording is provided to the City.

Phase 2: Construction & Rough-In

Structural Shell

Halli Construction completes all structural work, including foundation, framing, roofing, exterior walls, windows, and weatherproofing.

Interior Rough-In

Once framing is complete and walls are open, interior systems are installed and prepared for inspection. Halli Construction coordinates sequencing to ensure inspections occur at the proper time.

Rough-In Inspections

City inspectors verify that all rough-in work meets current code requirements before insulation or drywall may proceed.

Phase 3: Final Completion & Occupancy

Interior Finish & Trim

Halli Construction completes drywall, cabinetry, flooring, trim, fixtures, and all finish details required to complete the unit.

Final Inspection & Certificate of Occupancy

After final inspections are approved, the City issues a Certificate of Occupancy, allowing the ADU to be legally occupied.
